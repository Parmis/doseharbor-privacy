DoseHarbor Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 7, 2026

DoseHarbor is a medication reminder app for iPhone. This policy is short because there is genuinely nothing to disclose: DoseHarbor does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any data. Ever.

What data DoseHarbor stores

Everything you enter — medication names, strengths, notes, schedules, and your dose history — is stored only on your device, in the app’s private storage. It is never sent to us (we have no servers) or to anyone else.

What data DoseHarbor collects

None.

  • No account. There is nothing to sign up for.
  • No analytics. The app contains no analytics or tracking SDKs of any kind.
  • No ads. There are no ad networks in the app.
  • No third-party services. The app makes no network requests.

The App Store privacy label for DoseHarbor is “Data Not Collected.”

Notifications

Reminders are scheduled locally on your device using Apple’s notification system. Notification content (which can include medication names) never leaves your device. If you prefer not to show medication names on your lock screen, enable Discreet notifications in Settings — reminders will say “Time for your reminder” instead.

Your data, your control

  • Export: Settings → Export data (JSON) gives you a complete copy of everything the app stores.
  • Delete: Settings → Delete all data permanently removes everything, or simply delete the app — because data lives only on your device, deleting the app deletes the data.

Apple services

DoseHarbor currently stores data on-device only. If a future version adds optional iCloud backup, it will use your personal iCloud account under Apple’s terms and privacy policy; we will still have no access to your data, and this policy will be updated before that ships.

Children

DoseHarbor is not directed at children under 13 and collects no data from anyone, including children.

Not medical advice

DoseHarbor is a reminder tool, not medical advice. It does not diagnose, treat, or make recommendations about medications. Always follow your doctor’s or pharmacist’s guidance.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the new version will be posted at this address with an updated effective date. Since the app collects nothing, changes would only ever describe new optional features (like iCloud backup).
